Child's Pose (Balasana)

Hold 20-30 sec 3 repetitions ‚òÖ PAIN RELIEF

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start on your hands and knees on a comfortable mat or carpet
  2. Position knees slightly wider than your hips (about 6-8 inches apart)
  3. Turn your toes inward so big toes touch behind you
  4. Slowly push your hips backward toward your heels
  5. Extend your arms forward on the floor, palms down
  6. Allow your forehead to rest on the mat (use pillow if needed)
  7. Breathe deeply and feel gentle stretch in lower back
  8. Hold for 20-30 seconds, then slowly return to starting position

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

Child's Pose is your "emergency relief position" for lumbar stenosis. It creates maximum spinal flexion, which opens the spinal canal and immediately relieves pressure on compressed nerves. This position reduces the lumbar lordosis (backward curve) that narrows the canal when standing.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • If knees are uncomfortable, place a rolled towel behind them
  • If you can't reach the floor, rest on a pillow or bolster
  • Use this position ANY TIME pain increases - it's your go-to relief pose
  • You can gently rock side to side for added relief
‚ö†Ô∏è Caution: Stop if you experience sharp pain, dizziness, or difficulty breathing. If you have knee problems, use extra padding or modify the position.

Single Knee-to-Chest Stretch

Hold 15-20 sec 3 reps each leg BEGINNER

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lie flat on your back on a mat or firm surface
  2. Bend both knees, keeping feet flat on the floor
  3. Place both hands behind your right knee
  4. Slowly and gently pull your right knee toward your chest
  5. Keep your left foot planted firmly on the floor
  6. Hold the stretch without bouncing - steady pressure only
  7. Breathe normally throughout the hold
  8. Slowly lower right leg back to starting position
  9. Repeat with left leg

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

This stretch flexes the lumbar spine in a controlled, gentle manner. By pulling one knee at a time, you create targeted flexion that opens the spinal canal at specific levels. This position decompresses nerve roots and reduces pressure on the L3-L4 and L4-L5 discs.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • Perfect for doing in bed first thing in the morning!
  • Pull gently - you should feel a comfortable stretch, not pain
  • If you can't reach behind your knee, use a towel or belt
  • Keep your head and shoulders relaxed on the mat

Pelvic Tilt Exercise

Hold 5-10 sec 10-15 reps ‚òÖ CORE STRENGTH

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat on floor
  2. Place arms at sides, palms down
  3. Notice the natural arch in your lower back (you can slide hand under it)
  4. Tighten your abdominal muscles (like you're preparing for a punch)
  5. Simultaneously squeeze your buttocks together
  6. Push your lower back flat against the floor, eliminating the arch
  7. Your pelvis will tilt upward slightly
  8. Hold this position for 5-10 seconds while breathing normally
  9. Relax and return to neutral
  10. Repeat 10-15 times

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

This is THE MOST IMPORTANT exercise for real-world application! The pelvic tilt teaches you to actively flatten your spine using your core muscles. Once mastered lying down, you'll learn to do this while standing and walking - giving you an active tool to relieve stenosis symptoms throughout your day.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • Think "tuck your tailbone under" - this helps cue the movement
  • Your lower back should press into the floor - no gap!
  • Practice until you can do this without thinking about it
  • NEXT STEP: Learn to do this while standing (see Exercise 7)
What You'll Feel: Abdominal muscles and glutes working. Lower back pressing into floor.
Common Mistake: Don't just lift hips up - focus on flattening back to floor using muscle contraction.

Modified Cat-Cow (Cat Emphasis)

5 sec each 8-10 cycles MOBILITY

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start on hands and knees in tabletop position
  2. Hands directly under shoulders, knees under hips
  3. CAT POSE: Round your back up toward the ceiling like a scared cat
  4. Tuck your chin toward chest and tighten abdominal muscles
  5. HOLD CAT for 5-7 seconds (this is the therapeutic position!)
  6. COW POSE: Gently release back to neutral or slight arch
  7. Lift head to look forward (don't arch deeply or look up)
  8. HOLD COW for 2-3 seconds only
  9. Return to CAT pose
  10. Repeat 8-10 cycles, spending more time in CAT

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

The CAT portion (rounded back) creates spinal flexion that opens your spinal canal - this is where the therapeutic benefit comes from. The brief COW phase just adds gentle mobility. For stenosis, we emphasize CAT and minimize COW to avoid excessive extension.

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL MODIFICATION FOR STENOSIS: Unlike traditional cat-cow, DO NOT arch deeply into cow pose. Only go to neutral or slight arch. Deep extension (looking way up, belly dropping) will worsen stenosis symptoms!
üí° Pro Tips:
  • Spend 70% of time in CAT, 30% in neutral COW
  • Move slowly and smoothly - no jerking motions
  • If wrists hurt, try doing this on fists or use push-up handles
  • Coordinate movement with breathing: exhale in CAT, inhale in COW

Seated Spinal Flexion

Hold 10-15 sec 5 repetitions DESK-FRIENDLY

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair
  2. Place feet flat on floor, about hip-width apart
  3. Start with upright posture, shoulders relaxed
  4. Slowly bend forward from your waist, not your neck
  5. Let your arms hang loosely toward the floor
  6. Allow your upper body to relax completely
  7. Let your head hang naturally - don't force it down
  8. Breathe slowly and feel gentle stretch in lower back
  9. Hold for 10-15 seconds
  10. Slowly roll back up to sitting, one vertebra at a time

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

This is the perfect desk exercise you can do throughout your workday! The forward flexion opens the spinal canal while gravity gently assists the stretch. It counteracts the effects of prolonged sitting and provides immediate relief from stenosis symptoms.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • Do this every hour at your desk - set a timer!
  • Let gravity do the work - don't force yourself down
  • If you can't reach far, that's fine - just bend as far as comfortable
  • You can place hands on thighs and "walk" them down your legs
  • Perfect to do during phone calls or meetings
Perfect For: Office workers, anyone who sits long periods, quick relief during the day.
Bonus: Also stretches hamstrings and relieves lower back tension from sitting.

Standing Pelvic Tilt

Hold 10 sec 10 reps ‚òÖ REAL-WORLD

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Stand with your back against a wall
  2. Place feet about 12 inches from wall, shoulder-width apart
  3. Bend knees slightly (about 5-10 degrees)
  4. Notice the natural arch in your lower back (gap between back and wall)
  5. Tighten your abdominal muscles (pull belly button toward spine)
  6. Squeeze your buttocks together firmly
  7. Push your lower back flat against the wall (eliminate the gap)
  8. Hold this position for 10 seconds while breathing normally
  9. Maintain the muscle contraction - don't hold your breath!
  10. Step away from wall while maintaining this posture
  11. Practice walking with this pelvic tilt position

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

THIS IS YOUR SECRET WEAPON! Once you master this, you can actively relieve stenosis symptoms while standing and walking. This exercise teaches you to flatten your spine using muscle power instead of relying on external support. It's the bridge between lying-down exercises and real-world pain management.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • Master this against the wall first, then practice without wall support
  • Eventually you should be able to do this anywhere: standing in line, cooking, etc.
  • Think "tuck tailbone under" - this helps engage the right muscles
  • Your goal: maintain this posture ALL DAY while standing
  • At first your abs will get tired quickly - that's normal! Build endurance gradually
‚ö†Ô∏è Practice Schedule: Week 1: Against wall only, 10 seconds √ó 10 reps
Week 2: Against wall, then step away and hold 5 seconds
Week 3: Practice without wall, hold 10-15 seconds
Week 4+: Use constantly while standing and walking
The Goal: Transform this exercise into your default standing posture. Your core muscles replace mechanical support!
Success Marker: When you can maintain this posture while walking without thinking about it.

Wall Sits (Modified)

Hold 10-20 sec 3-5 reps STRENGTH

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Stand with back against a wall, feet hip-width apart
  2. Walk feet forward about 18-24 inches from wall
  3. Slowly slide down the wall by bending your knees
  4. START SHALLOW: Only bend to 45 degrees (not a deep squat!)
  5. Keep your back flat against the wall throughout
  6. Ensure knees stay behind or over toes (never past toes)
  7. Distribute weight through your heels, not toes
  8. Keep arms relaxed at sides or rest hands on thighs
  9. Hold this position for 10-20 seconds (or as long as comfortable)
  10. Slowly slide back up to standing
  11. Rest for 30 seconds between repetitions

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

Wall sits strengthen your quadriceps, glutes, and core muscles - all essential for maintaining proper posture that relieves stenosis. Strong legs allow you to walk longer with forward lean, and strong glutes help you maintain the pelvic tilt position during daily activities.

‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT MODIFICATIONS FOR STENOSIS:
  • Start with shallow bends (45¬∞) - don't go to full 90¬∞ squat initially
  • Keep back FLAT against wall - this maintains spinal flexion
  • If you feel stenosis pain increasing, you're going too deep - stay shallower
  • Progress depth gradually over weeks, not days
üí° Pro Tips:
  • Start with 10-second holds, build to 30+ seconds over several weeks
  • Breathe normally - don't hold your breath!
  • If thighs burn, that's normal - it means muscles are working
  • Stop if you feel sharp knee pain or stenosis symptoms increase
  • Can do this while watching TV or talking on phone
Progression: Week 1-2: 45¬∞ √ó 10 sec
Week 3-4: 60¬∞ √ó 15 sec
Week 5+: 90¬∞ √ó 20-30 sec
When to Stop: Quivering legs are OK. Sharp pain, stenosis symptoms, or knee pain = STOP.

Double Knee-to-Chest Stretch

Hold 20-30 sec 3 reps MAX RELIEF

Gentle
rocking Maximum spinal flexion - Most relief

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lie flat on your back on a mat or comfortable surface
  2. Bend both knees and place feet flat on floor
  3. Place both hands behind your knees (or on top of shins)
  4. Gently pull both knees toward your chest simultaneously
  5. Draw knees as close as comfortable - don't force it
  6. Keep your lower back pressed to the floor
  7. Relax your shoulders and neck
  8. Optional: Gently rock side to side for gentle back massage
  9. Hold this position for 20-30 seconds, breathing deeply
  10. Slowly release knees and return feet to floor

‚ú® Why This Exercise Helps

This provides maximum spinal flexion and opens the spinal canal to its widest possible dimension. It's one of the most effective positions for immediate stenosis relief. The gentle rocking motion can also release tension in the paraspinal muscles and provide a massage effect to the lower back.

üí° Pro Tips:
  • Perfect as the LAST exercise in your routine for maximum relief
  • Great to do in bed before sleeping or first thing in morning
  • If you can't hold behind knees, use a towel or strap around thighs
  • The rocking motion is optional but feels great!
  • You can hug knees tighter or looser based on comfort
When to Use: After other exercises, before bed, during pain flare-ups, or whenever you need maximum relief.
Pairs Well With: Child's pose - alternate between these two positions for prolonged relief sessions.
